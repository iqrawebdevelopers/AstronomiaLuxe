Craftsmanship
"The movement is finished as though the case will never be closed — because for us, it never truly is." Atelier Notebook, Vol. IV

Astronomia Luxe leaves the bench of a single watchmaker, not a production line. From the first bridge to the final tightened screw, one set of hands carries the movement through every stage — a discipline that costs weeks, but leaves no seam between hand and machine for a stranger to hide behind.

Perlage circles the mainplate in even, overlapping rings. Côtes de Genève run the length of each bridge, catching light differently at every angle of the wrist. Anglage is cut and polished by hand into every visible edge — a finish no CNC tool can replicate, because the reflective bevel it leaves depends on the varying pressure of a trained hand, not a fixed program.

The twin balance wheels beat in gentle counter-rotation, a visual signature as much as a mechanical one — you can watch the regulating organ breathe through the open dial, uninterrupted, for as long as you care to look.
